# Glossary

## Meridian Housing Group - Terms and Definitions

---

## Building Safety Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Accountable Person** | Under the Building Safety Act, a person (individual or organisation) who owns or has a legal obligation in relation to the common parts of a higher-risk building. Responsible for assessing building safety risks and managing them. |
| **Building Safety Case** | A document that demonstrates how a building's safety risks are being managed. Required for all higher-risk buildings under the Building Safety Act. |
| **Building Safety Manager** | Role appointed to manage building safety on a day-to-day basis. May be an employee or contractor. |
| **Building Safety Regulator (BSR)** | The regulator established under the Building Safety Act, part of the Health and Safety Executive, responsible for overseeing the safety and performance of all buildings. |
| **Golden Thread** | The requirement under the Building Safety Act to create, maintain, and provide access to accurate, up-to-date information about a building's design, construction, and ongoing management. Information must be stored digitally and be accessible throughout the building's lifecycle. |
| **Higher-Risk Building** | Under the Building Safety Act, a building that is at least 18 metres in height or has at least 7 storeys, and contains at least 2 residential units. Subject to additional regulatory requirements. |
| **Mandatory Occurrence Reporting** | The legal requirement for accountable persons to report certain safety occurrences to the Building Safety Regulator, including structural failures, spread of fire, and other prescribed events. |
| **Principal Accountable Person (PAP)** | Where there is more than one accountable person for a building, one must be designated as the principal accountable person with overall responsibility for compliance. |
| **Resident Engagement Strategy** | A documented approach to how residents will be kept informed about building safety and how their views will be sought and considered. Required under the Building Safety Act. |

## Compliance Certificate Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **ACM** | Asbestos Containing Material. Any material that contains asbestos fibres. |
| **CP12** | The common name for a Landlord Gas Safety Record, referring to form CP12 used by Gas Safe registered engineers. Valid for 12 months. |
| **EICR** | Electrical Installation Condition Report. An inspection and test of the fixed electrical installation in a property. For rented properties, must be carried out every 5 years. |
| **Fire Risk Assessment (FRA)** | A systematic evaluation of a building to identify fire hazards, people at risk, and measures to reduce or remove the risk. Required under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. |
| **Gas Safe Register** | The official list of gas engineers who are registered to work safely and legally on gas appliances in the UK. |
| **LOLER** | Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations 1998. Requires thorough examination of lifts at least every 6 months (for lifts carrying people). |

## Housing Management Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Housing Management System (HMS)** | Software used by housing organisations to manage properties, tenancies, rents, repairs, and other housing management functions. |
| **Northgate** | A leading housing management system vendor in the UK social housing sector. Meridian's core HMS. |
| **Secure Tenancy** | A type of tenancy offered by local authorities and housing associations that gives the tenant lifetime security of tenure. |
| **UPRN** | Unique Property Reference Number. A unique numeric identifier for every addressable location in Great Britain, managed by GeoPlace. |
| **Void** | A property that is currently empty (between tenancies) and not generating rental income. |

## Technology Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **API** | Application Programming Interface. A way for different software systems to communicate with each other. |
| **Bronze/Silver/Gold** | A data architecture pattern (medallion architecture) where Bronze contains raw data, Silver contains cleansed/conformed data, and Gold contains business-ready aggregations. |
| **Canvas App** | A type of Power App built with a drag-and-drop designer, suitable for custom user interfaces and mobile apps. |
| **Conditional Access** | Microsoft Entra ID feature that enforces access policies based on conditions like user location, device compliance, or risk level. |
| **Copilot Studio** | Microsoft's platform for building AI-powered conversational agents (chatbots) that can integrate with enterprise data. |
| **Dataverse** | Microsoft's cloud data platform, providing data storage and services that integrate with Power Platform and Dynamics 365. |
| **Document Intelligence** | Azure AI service (formerly Form Recognizer) that uses machine learning to extract text, key-value pairs, and tables from documents. |
| **ETL** | Extract, Transform, Load. The process of extracting data from source systems, transforming it to fit business needs, and loading it into a destination system. |
| **Lakehouse** | A data architecture that combines the benefits of data lakes (raw storage) and data warehouses (structured analytics) in one platform. |
| **Logic App** | Azure service for automating workflows and integrating apps, data, services, and systems. |
| **Microsoft Fabric** | Microsoft's unified analytics platform combining data lake, data warehouse, and data science capabilities. |
| **Model-Driven App** | A type of Power App built around data model and business logic, auto-generating the user interface from the data structure. |
| **PIM** | Privileged Identity Management. Azure AD feature providing just-in-time privileged access to reduce standing admin privileges. |
| **Power Pages** | Microsoft's low-code platform for building external-facing websites integrated with Dataverse. |
| **Private Endpoint** | Azure networking feature that brings Azure PaaS services into a virtual network using a private IP address. |
| **RAG** | Retrieval-Augmented Generation. AI pattern where relevant documents are retrieved from a knowledge base and provided to a language model to generate contextual responses. |
| **Semantic Model** | In Power BI, a data model that defines relationships, measures, and metadata for reporting. |
